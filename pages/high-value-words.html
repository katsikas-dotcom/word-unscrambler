High-Value Scrabble Words

Q, Z, X & J words every Scrabble player should know by heart

The four highest-value letters in Scrabble — Q (10pts), Z (10pts), X (8pts), J (8pts) — can swing a game by 20–50 points if played well. This guide covers the essential words using each letter, their point values, and expert tips for using them on the board.

Q

Scrabble Scoring Strategy

High-value tiles are only powerful when used strategically. Here are the principles that separate casual players from experts:

  • Triple Word Score + high tiles = massive plays. QUIZ on a TWS scores 93+ points
  • Don't hold high-value tiles "waiting for the perfect moment" — opportunities expire
  • Short Q-words (QI, QAT) let you dump the Q without needing a U
  • ZA and XI as parallel plays often score 20–30 points with no new tiles used
  • Double Letter Score under J or X in a short word (like JAB, BOX) is often better than a longer low-value word
